PhD Thesis

Currently, I am working on the Characterization of Magnetohydrodynamic Instabilities in Magnetized Plasmas through a Data-Driven Approach at Aix-Marseille University. Magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) is an interdisciplinary field combining fluid mechanics and electromagnetism to study the properties of conducting fluids subjected to magnetic fields. The mathematical description involves a complex system of nonlinear partial differential equations (PDEs), presenting significant challenges in understanding MHD instabilities. These instabilities are crucial for applications such as nuclear fusion (plasma stability in tokamaks) and astrophysical magnetic field dynamics. This thesis aims to address the characterization of MHD instabilities through mathematical modeling, numerical analysis, and the application of modern machine learning techniques to identify and predict unstable behaviors.
